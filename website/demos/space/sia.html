Sparse Ideal Atlas — primitive splits.
The seed atlas is 4 quadrant faces meeting at the origin, with ideal vertices at the cardinal directions. Each face owns its own copies of every vertex (un-shared); all edge transforms are identity so the canvas behaves visually like a flat plane.

Shift + click anywhere in the canvas to split the underlying face at that point via splitFaceAtInterior. Each split adds 2 faces and re-anchors them to the inserted point as their canonical (0, 0) origin. Atlas invariants are validated after every mutation.

Drag shapes around. They re-locate to whichever face contains their origin — watch the (N) counts next to each face label change as a shape crosses a face boundary.

Pan: two-finger scroll. Zoom: ⌘ / Ctrl + scroll.
